About

I am a Member of Technical Staff in Performance Engineering at Zyphra, focused on ML infrastructure for large-model training and serving. My current work spans training control-plane systems, distributed checkpointing and recovery, GPU systems reliability, and inference performance on AMD GPU clusters.

I built and operate Zookeeper, Zyphra's internal Slack-native control plane for large-scale LLM training, supporting job controls, automated rekicks, checkpoint sync management, node exclusion workflows, and real-time operator intervention during active training runs.

On reliability, I work on recovery paths for failure-prone multi-node training: pairwise NVMe checkpoint replication, failed-node replacement workflows, object-store checkpoint repair, monitoring incident response, and restart continuity for live LLM training runs.

My Ph.D. research at Penn applied ML to adaptive distributed systems, including reinforcement-learning and contextual-bandit policies for runtime blockchain execution, adaptive sharding under adversarial skew, and performance work on kernel-bypass TCP migration.

Current technical focus: training control planes, distributed checkpoints, AMD GPU systems reliability, MI355X inference benchmarking, ROCm/HIP debugging, Slurm, Prometheus, NVMe, and object-store recovery.

Technical Focus

My work is ML-heavy infrastructure: inference serving, distributed training, and ML-driven adaptivity.

  • ML Training Infrastructure: Slack-native job controls, automated rekicks, checkpoint sync management, node exclusion, restart orchestration, Slurm, NFS/NVMe, Prometheus, and Backblaze B2 workflows
  • LLM Inference: AMD MI355X benchmarking, TTFT/output-throughput analysis, vLLM serving, kernel behavior, RCCL/HIP stack performance, and high-throughput serving metrics
  • GPU Systems Reliability: ROCm/HIP memory anomalies, Prometheus storage incidents, monitoring regressions, cluster storage failures, root-cause analysis, and live workload remediation
  • ML for Systems: Reinforcement learning, contextual bandits, online policy selection, workload featurization, and adaptive execution for changing workloads
  • Performance Evaluation: Benchmark harnesses, profiling workflows, communication-pattern analysis, bottleneck detection, and regression workflows for production ML systems
