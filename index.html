About

I am a Member of Technical Staff in Performance Engineering at Zyphra, where I work on reliability and performance for large-scale GPU training and LLM inference systems. I completed my Ph.D. and M.S. in Computer and Information Science at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Prof. Boon Thau Loo.

My work spans distributed training orchestration, checkpoint validation and recovery, vLLM inference performance, speculative decoding, profiling, and production systems operations. I build systems that make large-scale infrastructure more reliable, adaptive, and easier to debug.

My research at Penn focused on adaptive distributed systems: reinforcement-learning based blockchain execution, adaptive sharding in untrusted environments, fault-tolerant data systems, and kernel-bypass TCP migration.

Explore my research projects and implementations, publications, or resume.

Current focus: distributed training reliability, checkpointing systems, and inference performance for large models.

Research Interests

My interests sit at the intersection of ML infrastructure, distributed systems, and performance engineering:

  • ML / AI Systems: Distributed training, checkpoint validation and recovery, LLM inference, speculative decoding, and vLLM performance
  • Performance Engineering: Profiling, bottleneck analysis, benchmark infrastructure, and production observability for large-scale systems
  • Adaptive Distributed Systems: Workload-aware sharding, runtime switching, consensus protocols, and fault-tolerant execution
  • Systems for Reliability: Recovery protocols, health monitoring, and operational tooling for high-availability infrastructure
